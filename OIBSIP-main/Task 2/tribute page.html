Rohit Sharma: A Cricketing Legend and Master of the Game - A Tribute

About the Legend


Rohit Sharma is a professional cricketer from India who is known for his aggressive and stylish batting. He was born on April 30, 1987, in Nagpur, Maharashtra, India.

Rohit made his debut in international cricket in 2007 and has since become one of the most successful and consistent batsmen in the Indian cricket team. Rohit Sharma is a right-handed batsman who is equally proficient in both Test and limited-overs cricket. He is known for his ability to score big runs and has several records to his name, including the highest individual score in ODIs (264) and the most number of double centuries in ODIs (3).

Besides his batting skills, Rohit is also a skilled fielder and can bowl off-spin when required. Rohit Sharma is widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world and is a key member of the Indian cricket team.
“THE HITMAN”

About the Career


☛ Rohit Sharma started his cricket career as a middle-order batsman for his domestic team, Mumbai. He made his first-class debut in 2006 and played a crucial role in Mumbai's victory in the Ranji Trophy that year. He made his international debut for India in June 2007 in an ODI match against Ireland.

☛ In his early years, Rohit Sharma struggled to establish himself in the Indian cricket team, but he gradually improved his performances and became a regular member of the team. He played a crucial role in India's victory in the 2007 World Twenty20, scoring an unbeaten 50 in the final against Pakistan.

☛ Over the years, Rohit Sharma has become one of the most successful and consistent batsmen in the Indian cricket team. He has played several memorable innings in all formats of the game and has helped India win many matches. He has also been a part of many historic moments in Indian cricket, including India's victory in the 2011 World Cup.

☛ He joined the Indian Premier League (IPL) in 2008 when he was signed by the Deccan Chargers franchise. Later, In the 2011 auction, he was sold for to the Mumbai Indians. He scored his only IPL century in the 2012 tournament with 109 (not out) against the Kolkata Knight Riders. Under his leadership, Mumbai has won the IPL in 2013, 2015, 2017, 2019 and 2020; they also won the former Champions League Twenty20 competition in 2013.

☛ He is currently (March 2022) one of six players who have scored 5,000 career runs in the competition. Sharma has 5,611 runs with one century and 40 half centuries and is the third-highest run scorer after Virat Kohli and Shikhar Dhawan .

About the Achievements

National honors

Sporting honors

-> ICC Men's ODI Cricketer of the Year: 2019
-> ICC Men's ODI Team of the Year: 2014 (12th man),2016, 2017, 2018, 2019
-> ICC Men's ODI Team of the Decade: 2011–2020
-> ICC Men's T20I Team of the Decade: 2011–2020
-> ICC Men's Test Team of the Year: 2021

other Achivements
-> Highest individual score in ODIs: Rohit Sharma holds the record for the highest individual score in ODIs, having scored an unbeaten 264 against Sri Lanka in 2014.
-> Most double centuries in ODIs: Rohit Sharma is the only cricketer to have scored three double centuries in ODIs, the first of which he scored against Australia in 2013.
-> Fastest T20I century by an Indian: Rohit Sharma holds the record for the fastest century by an Indian in T20Is, having scored one off 35 balls against Sri Lanka in 2017.
-> Most runs in a single edition of the World Cup: Rohit Sharma scored 648 runs in the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup, the most by any batsman in a single edition of the tournament.
-> IPL titles: Rohit Sharma has led the Mumbai Indians team to five IPL titles as captain, the most by any captain in the tournament's history.
For his achievements on India's tour of England in 2021, Sharma was selected by Wisden Cricketers' Almanack as one of the five Wisden Cricketers of the Year in its 2022 edition.

☛ Sharma engages in numerous philanthropic activities, promoting various causes such as animal welfare, health, and children. He is particularly vocal about the protection of animals and has supported various initiatives and organisations to promote the cause.

☛ In February 2015, Sharma joined People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA) to support sterilisation of homeless cats and dogs. When supporting the cause Sharma said, "Sterilisation is important because I feel that if we can stop (the homeless-animal crisis), there will be control of population among the street dogs"
